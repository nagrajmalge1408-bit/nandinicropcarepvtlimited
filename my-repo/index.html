Why PGRs?

Understanding Plant Growth Regulators

Plant Growth Regulators are naturally occurring or synthetic compounds that, in small quantities, alter the physiological processes in plants. Unlike fertilisers, PGRs don't feed plants — they signal them to grow better.

  • Auxins regulate cell elongation, root formation, and apical dominance — key to transplant success and propagation.
  • Cytokinins stimulate cell division and delay senescence, improving fruit set and post-harvest shelf life.
  • Gibberellins promote stem elongation, seed germination, and can trigger flowering in biennial plants.
  • Ethylene regulators synchronise harvest, improve fruit colour, and manage crop maturity.
  • Retardants (anti-GAs) reduce lodging in cereals, compact canopies, and direct energy to reproductive growth.
